You take a beautiful photo on your iPhone, send it to your Windows PC or upload it to a website, and... "File not supported." Or you see a blank icon with the file extension .heic. What is this file, and why isn't it a JPG?

What is HEIC?

HEIC stands for High-Efficiency Image Format. Starting with iOS 11, Apple made this the default format for taking photos on iPhones and iPads. Why? Because it's better than JPG in almost every way.

  • Smaller File Size: A HEIC photo can be up to 50% smaller than the same photo saved as a JPG, with the same (or better) quality.
  • More Features: It can store multiple images in one file (like Live Photos), supports transparency like a PNG, and can store 16-bit color (vs. JPG's 8-bit).

It's a superior format. But it has one major problem: compatibility. Most of the world, including many web browsers, Windows applications, and online forms, still expects the universal JPG format.

Here are the easiest ways to deal with HEIC files.

Solution 1: Change Your iPhone Setting (The Permanent Fix)

If you *never* want to deal with HEIC files again and prefer your phone to just save JPGs, you can change a setting.

‚úÖ How to Make Your iPhone Save JPGs:

  1. Go to Settings on your iPhone.
  2. Scroll down and tap on Camera.
  3. Tap on Formats.
  4. Change the setting from "High Efficiency" (which is HEIC) to "Most Compatible" (which is JPG).

That's it. From now on, all photos you take will be saved as JPGs. The downside is they will take up more storage space on your device.

Solution 2: Convert HEIC Files as You Need Them (The "On-the-Fly" Fix)

What if you want to keep shooting in the high-efficiency HEIC format but just need to convert one or two photos for a website or a colleague? You can use a converter.

The problem is that most online "HEIC to JPG" converters require you to upload your personal photos to their servers. You have no idea who sees them, how long they are stored, or if they are sold. For personal photos, this is a major privacy risk.

Solution 3: Use Your Phone's "Share" or "Files" App

A simple workaround is to use your phone's built-in sharing. When you "share" a HEIC photo via email or many other apps, iOS often converts it to a JPG automatically.

  1. Open the HEIC photo in your Photos app.
  2. Tap the "Share" icon (the box with an arrow pointing up).
  3. Tap "Copy Photo".
  4. Now, open a new email or note, and "Paste" the photo. The pasted version will often be a JPG.
  5. Alternatively, save the photo to your "Files" app. Often, this simple act of "saving" it will convert the format.

While HEIC is technically the future, JPG is still the present. Knowing how to quickly convert between them is an essential skill for any iPhone user.
